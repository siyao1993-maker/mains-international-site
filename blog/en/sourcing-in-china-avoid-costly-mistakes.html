Sourcing in China: how to avoid costly mistakes

Practical points for European companies that want to find, verify and manage suppliers in China before committing investment.

Introduction

China remains a strong manufacturing market, but sourcing is rarely just “finding a supplier”. The real value lies in selection, verification and follow-up. Without local checks, small misunderstandings can turn into delays, quality issues or unexpected costs.

Do not start with price, start with fit

The cheapest quotation is not automatically the best choice. First check whether the supplier fits the product, required volumes, certification, quality expectations and export experience. A factory that performs well in one product category may be unsuitable for another.

Verify before you order

Do not only ask for documents; check whether the information is reliable. Consider business registration, production capacity, reference products, certificates and communication quality. A short local verification can reduce risk before money or time is committed.

Define specifications very concretely

Many problems occur because product specifications are too general. Work with clear drawings, materials, tolerances, packaging requirements, quality criteria and acceptance limits. What one party calls “premium” may be standard quality to another.

Follow up during production

Quality control after production is often too late. It is better to build in fixed checkpoints during production. Deviations become visible earlier, and adjustments can still be made before the full batch is completed.

Conclusion
