Importing from China: what should you watch for?

An overview of key points around documentation, logistics, customs and communication when importing from China.

Introduction

Importing from China can create strong opportunities, but it requires proper preparation. The biggest risks are often not in transport itself, but in unclear agreements, missing documents or different expectations around timing and responsibility.

Make Incoterms explicit

Agree exactly who is responsible for transport, insurance, export documents, customs and transfer of risk. Incoterms such as FOB, CIF or EXW may look standard, but are often interpreted differently in practice. Confirm these agreements in writing.

Check documents early

The commercial invoice, packing list, HS code, certificates and transport documents need to be correct. Errors can lead to delays, additional costs or customs issues. Check documents before shipment, not only when goods arrive.

Look beyond freight costs

The total landed cost consists of more than product price and freight. Include import duties, VAT, storage, inspections, insurance, local handling and possible delays. Only then do you get a realistic view of margin and risk.

Communication is part of logistics

Time zones, language, culture and different working methods can create delays. One local point of contact that connects suppliers, logistics providers and European stakeholders makes the process calmer and more predictable.

Conclusion
